The success of a software like Netflix is largely based on its ability to meet user needs through a user-friendly interface. In the requirements gathering stage, the development team works to understand what the users really want by preparing well thought questions to capture customer requirements and ideas. After gathering initial responses, follow up questions may be asked to clarify details. The team then lists the identified requirements by their priority, and examines any prerequisites that may need to be addressed before moving forward. This stage is crucial because it sets the foundation for the software’s design, features, and functionality. A well executed requirements gathering process ensures that the final product satisfies the users’ needs and enhances their overall experience, increasing the chances of the software’s success.

User Stories

1. As a user with visual impairments, I want to have the screen reader compatibility and audio descriptions for content, so that I can easily navigate through the interface and enjoy shows and movies.

2. As a parent, I want to set up a kid-friendly profile with parental controls, so that I can be sure that my children will only see appropriate content.

3. As a frequent traveler, I want the ability to download content, so that I can watch shows and movies at the times that I do not have internet access.

4. As a Netflix administrator, I want to monitor streaming quality across different regions, so that I can address performance issues in real-time.

5. As a user learning a new language, I want to choose audio and subtitle options in different languages, so that I can practice listening and reading while enjoying content.
